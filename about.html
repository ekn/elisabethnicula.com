About

Elisabeth Nicula is an interdisciplinary artist from Norfolk, Virginia. Her work is in conversation with nature: she is interested in the sense of self engendered by landscapes; in observing and depicting the natural world via abstracted scales of space and time; and in poetics and memory. Smooth Friend is her new publishing project, and she also self-publishes websites composed of painstakingly-made animated GIFs, video footage, digital drawings, and photography. dioramas.space, her ongoing project supported by a grant from Rhizome, is about seeing and being seen by the non-human world, maintenance and tending, and connecting her emotional landscape to the exigencies of ecological disaster.

A funny thing happened in 2017. Elisabeth noticed a molting scrub jay in the backyard and started photographing him. She distrusts the idea of a daily practice but over the course of two years amassed over 30,000 photographs of one bird. He is gone now and she isn't sure what to do with his corpus.
